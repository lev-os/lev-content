## Body

Headcount doesn’t buy you coordination. What buys you coordination is a control plane you can read: which step ran, which gate failed, which patch was proposed, and what shipped. Without that, “multi-agent” is just parallel chaos with better excuses.

FlowMind is our answer at the substrate layer — declarative workflows, deterministic steps where the contract matters, and a path for skills to harden into programs as fidelity tightens. Markdown skills and YAML definitions aren’t rivals; they’re the same ladder at different resolutions. The point isn’t more autonomy; it’s inspectable autonomy.

This is the same instinct as the manifesto: understanding is the proposal, alignment is the gate, execution follows a declared change. Orchestration wins when the loop is legible — when someone on call can answer “what step failed?” without reading three DM threads.

We’re in the Web Summit runway. I’m less interested in agent fashion than in whether your stack can show receipts when the floor asks hard questions.
